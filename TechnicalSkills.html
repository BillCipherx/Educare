Technical Skills

What you'll learn

Learn to improve your technical photography skills with our photography courses in Educare College!

Photography is both a creative and a technical art form, the two go hand in hand. Without the creative vision, the technical skills are useless, and without the practical skills, the vision is just a dream. Once you have mastered the basics of your camera functions, all beginner photographers need to be able to master the technical aspects of their camera to make their creative vision come to life.

Perhaps you want a shot to look a certain way but you don't know how to light it, or you may want to achieve a certain depth of field but can't seem to replicate your idea in the viewfinder. This technical photography course takes your beginners knowledge and adds the tools of lighting, metering, correct exposure, motion blur and a helpful understanding of the various camera accessories at your disposal and emboldens any budding amateur to take that next leap into taking images with real impact.

The aim of this photography course is to combine theoretical and practical knowledge to enable you to approach a shot with more proficiency and confidence. If you're looking to grow your skills as an amateur then this photography course is for you!

Learning outcomes

By the end of this Technical Skills course, you should be able to:

  • Control light quantity and quality with in-camera methods
  • Apply advanced techniques for controlled exposure variations
  • Integrate camera operational control with specified creative output
  • Use light quality attributes to enhance photographic impact
  • Apply simple resolution processes to complex colour control issues
  • Extend the diversity of options for scene interpretation and technical capture

    • Course content

    What will be covered in this Technical Skills course?

  • Bracketing & Auto-exposure bracketing
  • Tonal Range & Dynamic Range
  • High Dynamic Range (HDR)
  • Lens filters
  • Histograms & exposures
  • High key / low key exposures
